# Echo Cave Support

Echo Cave is an offline, audio-first cave exploration game for iPhone, designed with blind players at its heart.

Public support page: <https://echo-cave.suliemanhaidari.chatgpt.site/support>

## Contact

For help, bug reports, accessibility feedback, or feature suggestions, email [vidalsulieman@gmail.com](mailto:vidalsulieman@gmail.com).

Support is provided in English. Please do not include passwords, financial information, health information, or other sensitive personal data.

## Quick start

1. Headphones are recommended so left and right directional sounds are easiest to distinguish.
2. Launch Echo Cave and activate **Enter Echo Cave**.
3. Listen to the introduction and tutorial, then activate **Begin**.
4. Use **Listen** whenever you want to hear the current room and available paths again.
5. Use the labeled movement controls to move forward, retreat, left, or right.
6. Use **Teleport Home** if you lose your way, and **Repeat Narration** if you missed a spoken message.

The first cave is a straight teaching path. Reach its exit to unlock deeper caves with branching passages.

## VoiceOver

- Turn on VoiceOver with your configured Accessibility Shortcut or in iPhone Settings → Accessibility → VoiceOver.
- Use normal VoiceOver navigation to find a control, then double-tap to activate it.
- All common game actions are intended to be available as labeled controls or accessibility actions. Optional game gestures are shortcuts and should not be required while VoiceOver is on.
- Screen Curtain can remain on during play.
- If the in-game narrator and VoiceOver overlap, wait for the current speech to finish and use **Repeat Narration**. Please report repeated or missing speech so it can be fixed.

Read the current [Echo Cave accessibility statement](docs/app-store/accessibility.md) for supported features, known scope, and testing commitments.

## Audio troubleshooting

If you cannot hear the cave:

1. Confirm media volume is audible and the intended speaker or headphones are connected.
2. Open Echo Cave Settings and confirm spoken narration and the desired sound cues are on.
3. Pause other audio, then return to Echo Cave and activate **Listen**.
4. Disconnect and reconnect Bluetooth headphones if iOS changed the audio route.
5. Lock and unlock the iPhone, reopen Echo Cave, and activate **Repeat Narration**.
6. If the problem continues, relaunch the app. Your saved progress should remain on the device.

Please report any sudden volume increase, persistent silence, reversed direction, speech overlap, or failure after Siri, a call, an alarm, locking, or changing headphones.

## Offline play and privacy

Echo Cave does not require an account or network connection for game play. Progress and settings are stored on the iPhone. Read the [Echo Cave Privacy Policy](PRIVACY.md) for details.

## Resetting or deleting progress

A reset action inside Echo Cave changes only the data named in its confirmation message. Read that message carefully before continuing.

To remove all Echo Cave data stored by the app on an iPhone, delete the app from that iPhone. Because Echo Cave has no account or cloud save, the developer cannot restore deleted local progress.

## What to include in a report

Include as much of the following as you can:

- Echo Cave version and build, if shown.
- iPhone model and iOS version.
- Whether VoiceOver, Screen Curtain, Mono Audio, a braille display, or other accessibility settings were active.
- Audio route: built-in speaker, wired headphones, Bluetooth headphones, or hearing device.
- The screen or cave action you were using.
- Steps that reproduce the problem.
- What you expected and what happened instead.
- Whether relaunching changed the result.

For an accessibility failure, please say whether the task required sighted help, focus became trapped or moved behind a dialog, a control was unlabeled, or a meaningful sound lacked a text, speech, or haptic alternative.

## Safety

Use a comfortable listening volume and stay aware of your surroundings. Do not play while driving or whenever game audio could prevent you from hearing important environmental sounds.
